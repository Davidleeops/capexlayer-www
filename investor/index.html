The Problem

Enterprise Sales Enters Too Late

Major capital projects generate detectable signals months before procurement begins. Those signals exist today, scattered across permits, regulatory filings, infrastructure queues, corporate disclosures, and government procurement databases.

No one is connecting them. Enterprise vendors discover opportunities after the RFP drops, when competition is already at its peak and relationships are locked. The information exists. The infrastructure to act on it does not.

The Solution

Industrial Business Intelligence

CapexLayer is a signal intelligence platform purpose-built for capital project markets. It monitors 52 data sources continuously, detects early project signals, and maps the full decision structure behind each one: who the sponsor is, who controls the budget, and when the project will move into execution.

That intelligence is delivered into the buyer's workflow, formatted as an actionable account brief, not a raw data dump. Teams enter conversations armed with context their competitors do not have.

This is a new category: industrial business intelligence for capital deployment. It sits upstream of construction platforms, enterprise workflow systems, and industrial analytics tools. It feeds them all.

Long-term vision

The Intelligence Network for Capital Deployment

Infrastructure, energy, data centers, advanced manufacturing, and industrial construction collectively represent tens of trillions in annual global capital spending. Every dollar of that capital leaves a signal trail before it moves.

CapexLayer's long-term goal is to be the canonical intelligence layer for that capital, the system of record for where industrial money is going, who controls it, and what it will fund. The vertical wedge is cranes and construction. The platform ceiling is the global industrial economy.

Build

Product Architecture

Signal Engine
Continuously monitors 52 data sources, permits, regulatory filings, EDGAR disclosures, utility queues, and procurement notices, and scores each signal by project stage and capital scale.
Decision Intelligence
Identifies the investors, developers, and executives who control each project. Maps org structure, budget authority, and procurement timeline into a single account profile.
Money Flow Graph
Traces how capital moves from fund to developer to general contractor to vendor. Reveals who gets paid, when, and what relationships to prioritize first.
Simulation Layer
Models how projects evolve across procurement cycles and supply chain constraints. Surfaces the optimal entry point for each enterprise account.
TAM

Market Opportunity

CapexLayer's immediate market is the enterprise sales teams that sell into capital-intensive industries. Any company whose customers spend capital on projects is a potential subscriber.

Data Centers Infrastructure Energy & Utilities Industrial Construction Advanced Manufacturing

The platform operates upstream of the entire capital project ecosystem. It is not a construction tool or a CRM. It is the layer that tells enterprise vendors where to look before anyone else knows a project exists. The serviceable market includes equipment vendors, staffing firms, specialty contractors, project finance institutions, surety bond companies, and industrial software providers, each of which pays to acquire project-level intelligence today through slower, more expensive means.
